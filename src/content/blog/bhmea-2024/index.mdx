Finally I have some free time to write about my trip to Saudi Arabia for Blackhat MEA, which happened in November 2024 üíÄ

I came to the event as a member of `snibedisnaphesback` and got full reimbursements for being one of the top 10 teams in the qualification (to be exact, we were the eleventh but `The Flat Network Society` withdrew).

As a fellow Vietnamese, `Onirique` from `thehackerscrew` also came along with me on the same flights and we both shared one hotel room in Riyadh.

Information about Blackhat MEA 2024 can be found [here](https://blackhatmea.com).

## First impression

idk why but I had to take flights from Ho Chi Minh City to Hong Kong and then from Hong Kong to Riyadh, which was a very odd route...

![](images/route.png)

After arriving in Riyadh, we took a loooong 40km trip from the King Khalid Int'l Airport to our hotel, Boudl AL Sulaymaniyah. 

![](images/hotel.jpg)

They acquired us a very big apartment-like accommodation with a living room and a twin room, with greatly prepared amenities.

## Main event (Day 1 - 2)

We took a 100km road trip by bus from the hotel to get to the onsite location (which was held in the middle of a desert üíÄ).

Before the start of the CTF, I wandered around to check the booths of the conference. There were many exciting sectors, such as airplane hacking, car hacking, medical security, ...

<div class="mx-auto w-1/2">

This below was the atmosphere of the CTF event, with over 250 teams participated. Everytime someone first-blooded a chall, or a new team got the first position, they would play an audio (I did not flinch) and show the team's name on that big screen.

<div class="mx-auto w-1/2">
    ![](images/day1_1.jpg)

The challenges were okayish, except the one that reused the obfuscation scheme from Flare-on 11 Challenge 9 (Serpentine) on an infeasible algorithm that needed to be guessed to solve, which annoyed me a little bit.

## Al-Nassr vs. Damac

I didn't expect the ticket for this football match at Al-Awwal Park (home stadium of Al-Nassr) to be super cheap - we bought 2 tickets for seats in the corner of the stadium for `~15$` each.

<div class="mx-auto w-1/2">
    ![](images/football.jpg)
</div>

Before the match, we also paid for some French fries with cheese and a signature sauce as dressings, and it was spot on.

This was my first time attending a live match outside of Vietnam, and I was amazed by Al-Nassr fans! They cheered very actively without feeling tired throughout the whole match.

We were so lucky WTF? Our semi-üêê Cristiano Ronaldo scored a penalty at 17' and ran straight into our face, celebrated with his signature moves. SIUUUUUUUUUUUU!!!

<YouTube id="https://youtube.com/shorts/gGNNUaQVkBk" />

He then later scored his second goal at 79' and just barely missed his hat-trick by being offsided. What a match!

## Random things

### JLPT

I skipped the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) level N2 in order to enjoy the whole trip to Saudi Arabia üíÄ Gotta wait until July 2025 for the next exam window üíÄ

### University assignment

Me and Onirique had to work on an assignment for 24 hours straight before the football match üíÄ We bought the tickets before starting the assignment to boost ourselves up üíÄ

### Traffic in Saudi

First time I have been on a 135km/h car (average in Vietnam is like 40km/h). The roads were also poorly designed at the intersections as well (~~no wonder 90% of cars in Saudi were dented~~ üíÄ)

### Thesis instructor

What a coincidence that he also went to Saudi for Blackhat MEA conference. I intended to take a week off from thesis, but ended up having to work onsite with him in the hotel üíÄ

## Time to go back
